⭐ The STAR Framework (Master this first)
S — Situation
Set the context. Where were you? What was the project? Keep it to 2-3 sentences.
T — Task
What was your specific responsibility? What was expected of you?
A — Action
What did YOU do? Use "I" not "we". This is 60% of your answer. Be specific.
R — Result
What was the outcome? Use numbers. "Reduced latency by 40%", "Saved 2 hours/week".
Pro Tips

Keep each story to 2-3 minutes. Prepare 10-15 stories covering all themes. Practice aloud. Record yourself and review.

📋 Amazon Leadership Principles (16 LPs)
1. Customer Obsession
Start with the customer and work backwards. Earn and keep customer trust.
2. Ownership
Think long term. Act on behalf of the entire company. Never say "that's not my job."
3. Invent and Simplify
Seek new ideas from everywhere. Find ways to simplify.
4. Are Right, A Lot
Strong judgment and good instincts. Seek diverse perspectives.
5. Learn and Be Curious
Never done learning. Always seek to improve yourself.
6. Hire and Develop the Best
Raise the performance bar. Develop leaders.
7. Insist on Highest Standards
Continually raise the bar. Drive quality.
8. Think Big
Create and communicate a bold direction that inspires results.
9. Bias for Action
Speed matters. Many decisions are reversible. Don't over-analyze.
10. Frugality
Accomplish more with less. Constraints breed resourcefulness.
11. Earn Trust
Listen attentively, speak candidly, treat others respectfully.
12. Dive Deep
Operate at all levels. Stay connected to the details.
13. Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit
Challenge decisions respectfully. Once decided, commit wholly.
14. Deliver Results
Focus on key inputs. Deliver them with the right quality and timely fashion.
15. Strive to be Earth's Best Employer
Create a safer, more diverse, more productive work environment.
16. Success and Scale Bring Broad Responsibility
Be humble. Make the world better for future generations.

🏢 Company-Specific Behavioral Tips (4 companies)
Google — "Googleyness"
Focus on: intellectual humility, doing the right thing, working well with diverse teams. They want collaborative, curious, and principled engineers. Avoid ego.
Microsoft — Growth Mindset
Focus on: learning from mistakes, empathy, customer obsession, collaborative spirit. "How did you grow from this experience?" is key. Show curiosity and adaptability.
Amazon — Leadership Principles
Every behavioral question maps to 1-2 LPs. Prepare 2 stories per LP. Use data and metrics heavily. "Disagree and Commit" and "Dive Deep" are favorites for SDE-2+.
Meta — "Move Fast"
Focus on: speed of execution, impact at scale, handling ambiguity. They value engineers who ship fast, iterate, and aren't afraid of breaking things (responsibly).
